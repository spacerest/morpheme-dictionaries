# Prefixes
o- | un-, not | The Swedish equivalent of English "un-" or "in-", stuck onto adjectives to flip their meaning. It's satisfyingly predictable: olycklig means unhappy, omöjlig means impossible, ofarlig means harmless. If you know the base word, slap on o- and you've got the opposite.
be- | (makes verb transitive) | Borrowed from the same Germanic root as English "be-" in words like "befriend" or "beset". In Swedish it turns nouns and adjectives into verbs or makes intransitive verbs more direct: betala means to pay, beställa means to order, behöva means to need. Very common in everyday speech.
för- | forward, too much, away | A versatile prefix with cousins in English "fore-" and "for-". It can mean forward in time (förbereda, to prepare), intensify an action to excess (försova sig, to oversleep), or add a sense of transformation (förändra, to change). One of the most common prefixes you will meet.
ut- | out, outward | Directly mirrors English "out-" in both form and meaning. Utgång means exit (out-going), utbyte means exchange (out-swap), utomhus means outdoors. Because it maps so cleanly onto English, this one clicks fast for learners.
upp- | up, upward | The Swedish twin of English "up-" used as a verbal particle or prefix. Upplevelse means experience (literally an up-living), uppgift means task or assignment, uppehåll means a pause or stay. You will spot it constantly in compound verbs.
av- | off, away, down | Matches English "off" or "away". Avgång means departure, avsluta means to finish (close off), avstånd means distance. It often signals separation or completion of an action, so it gives useful context clues when reading new words.
sam- | together, co- | The Swedish counterpart of English "co-" or "together". Samarbete means cooperation, samhälle means society (literally a together-holding), tillsammans means together. Recognizing sam- instantly tells you the word involves joint action or shared space.
mis- | wrongly, mis- | A near-identical match to English "mis-", both in spelling and meaning. Missförstånd means misunderstanding, misslyckas means to fail (to mis-succeed), misstag means mistake. Because it looks and sounds like English, it is one of the easiest Swedish prefixes to internalize.
# Suffixes
-ning | (action noun) | Attaches to verb stems to create nouns describing a process or its result, much like English "-ing" used as a gerund or "-tion". Utbildning means education, förändring means change, betalning means payment. It is one of the most productive noun-forming suffixes in the language.
-het | -ness, -ity | Creates abstract nouns from adjectives, just like English "-ness" or "-ity". Frihet means freedom (fri + het), säkerhet means safety or security, möjlighet means possibility. Once you know the adjective, the noun is free.
-lig | -ly, -able, -ful | Turns nouns and verbs into adjectives, overlapping with English "-ly", "-able", and "-ful" depending on context. Farlig means dangerous, möjlig means possible, vänlig means friendly. It is extremely common and learning it unlocks dozens of adjectives at once.
-ig | -y, -ish | A lighter adjectival suffix similar to English "-y" or "-ish", attached to nouns to mean "having the quality of". Hungrig means hungry, krånglig means tricky or fiddly, dammig means dusty. The connection to English "-y" makes the pattern feel intuitive.
-are | -er, person who | The standard suffix for agent nouns, just like English "-er". Lärare means teacher, läkare means doctor (one who heals), förare means driver. The doubling of the final r in some words is a quirk but the meaning stays rock solid.
-ande/-ende | (present participle) | Forms the present participle, equivalent to English "-ing" on a verb. Intressant is borrowed, but forms like leende (smiling), spännande (exciting), and talande (speaking) are built with this suffix. It can also function as an adjective, so spännande film means exciting film.
-skap | -ship, -hood | Creates nouns about relationships, states, or collections, mirroring English "-ship" and "-hood". Vänskap means friendship, vetenskap means science (knowledge-ship), medborgarskap means citizenship. The English cognate "-ship" makes this one memorable.
-tion/-sion | -tion, -sion | Borrowed from Latin via French, just as in English, and pronounced roughly "shoon" in Swedish. Nation, situation, and information look almost identical to their English counterparts. The spelling is your biggest clue that the word is an international loanword.
-else | (abstract noun) | Turns verbs into abstract nouns expressing a state or result, with no perfect English equivalent but a feel similar to "-ence" or "-ment". Känsla (feeling) is related, and clearer examples include upplevelse (experience), förändelse (hmm, förändring is more common), and händelse (event). It adds a slightly more emotional or experiential tone than -ning.
-lös | -less, without | The Swedish mirror of English "-less", tacked onto nouns to mean lacking something. Maktlös means powerless, smärtfri is pain-free but smärtlös also exists, hjälplös means helpless. The resemblance to "loose" in English is a handy memory hook even if the etymology differs.
# Roots and stems
-lev-/-liv- | life, live | From the old Germanic root for life and living, related to English "live" and "life". Leva means to live, liv means life, livsstil means lifestyle. You will find this root tucked into many compound words wherever life or living is the theme.
-arbete- | work | Directly cognate with English "work" at a deeper level and transparent in meaning on its own. Arbete means work, arbeta means to work, arbetslös means unemployed. It compounds freely: hemarbete is homework in the sense of housework, samarbete is cooperation.
-tid- | time | Related to English "tide" in its older sense of a period of time. Tid means time, tidning means newspaper (originally a report of the times), fritid means leisure time. Spotting tid- inside a word reliably signals something to do with time or a period.
-hand- | hand | A direct cognate with English "hand", and it compounds just as productively. Handel means trade or commerce (handling of goods), handväska means handbag, handskas means to handle or deal with. Because it looks like English, learners pick it up almost automatically.
-barn- | child | Cognate with the Scottish and Northern English dialect word "bairn", meaning child. Barn means child or children (it is the same form for both), barndom means childhood, barnvakt means babysitter. Knowing this root unlocks a cluster of family and care vocabulary.
-hus- | house | An exact cognate with English "house". Hus means house, sjukhus means hospital (sick-house), rådhus means town hall. Swedish loves to build compounds this way, and hus- is one of the most frequent building blocks you will encounter in everyday vocabulary.
-fri- | free, freedom | Cognate with English "free" and related to the name Friday (Frigg's day). Fri means free, frihet means freedom, fritid means free time or leisure. It appears in compounds to indicate something is without cost or without restriction, much as English uses "free" in "sugar-free" or "carefree".
