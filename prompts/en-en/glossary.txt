# Prefixes -- Germanic/Old English
un- | un-/not | The most common English negating prefix, inherited from Old English. Directly mirrors German un-, Dutch on-, and relatives across Germanic languages. Turns the meaning of the base word on its head: unhappy, unknown, unforgettable.
re- | re-/again | Signals repetition or a return to a previous state. From Latin re-, it appears in thousands of English words: return, rebuild, reconsider. Often interchangeable with "doing again" or "going back."
over- | over-/too much | Suggests excess or going beyond a limit. Overlook, overwhelm, overestimate. Also used spatially for "above" in older compounds.
under- | under-/too little | The counterpart to over-. Can mean below (underground) or not enough (underestimate, underperform).
out- | out-/beyond | Points outward or signals surpassing something: outnumber, outstanding, outrun.
mis- | mis-/wrongly | Signals that something was done incorrectly or badly. Misunderstand, mistake, misrepresent.
fore- | fore-/before | Before or in front. Forecast, foresee, forehead.

# Prefixes -- Latin
in-/im-/il-/ir- | in-/not or into | A chameleon prefix with two distinct meanings: "not" (inactive, impossible, illegal) and "into" (insert, import). The spelling shifts to match the following sound.
ex-/e-/ef- | ex-/out of | Out of, former, or away. Exhale, export, eject, ex-president.
con-/com-/co-/col-/cor- | con-/together | With or together. One of the most productive Latin prefixes. Connect, combine, cooperate, collect. The spelling varies to match the following sound.
de- | de-/down/undo | Downward, away, or reversing an action. Decrease, depart, deconstruct. Often directly reverses the base word.
dis- | dis-/not/apart | Not, or taking apart. Disagree, disconnect, disappear. Stronger sense of separation or reversal than un-.
pre- | pre-/before | Before in time or position. Predict, prepare, prehistoric.
pro- | pro-/forward/for | Forward, in favor of, or in place of. Progress, produce, pronoun.
sub- | sub-/under | Under, below, or secondary. Subway, submarine, substitute.
inter- | inter-/between | Between or among. International, interrupt, interact.
trans- | trans-/across | Across, through, or changing from one state to another. Transport, transform, translate.
super- | super-/above | Above, over, or exceptional. Superman, superior, supernatural.
anti- | anti-/against | Against or opposing. Antiwar, antibiotic, anticipate.
per- | per-/through | Through, thoroughly, or completely. Perform, permit, persist.
ad-/a- | to/toward | Toward or in addition to. Adapt, admit, attract, arrive.

# Suffixes
-tion/-sion | -tion | The most common English noun suffix from Latin. Turns verbs into nouns naming an action or its result. Construction, permission, explosion. Equivalent to German -ung.
-ment | -ment | Forms nouns from verbs, referring to the action, its result, or its means. Government, movement, enjoyment.
-ness | -ness | Turns adjectives into nouns meaning a state or quality. Happiness, darkness, awareness. The Germanic equivalent of Latin -ity.
-ity/-ty | -ity | The Latin counterpart to -ness. Forms abstract nouns from adjectives. Equality, electricity, reality.
-er/-or | -er | Forms agent nouns -- the person or thing performing the action. Teacher, actor, driver, computer.
-ist | -ist | A person who practices, specializes in, or believes in something. Artist, scientist, pianist.
-ism | -ism | A belief, practice, system, or characteristic quality. Realism, capitalism, enthusiasm.
-ize/-ise | -ize | To make something have a quality, or to cause a process. Modernize, organize, realize.
-ify | -ify | To make or cause to become. Simplify, clarify, intensify.
-ful | -ful | Full of or characterized by. Beautiful, careful, powerful.
-less | -less | Without. Careless, hopeless, speechless.
-able/-ible | -able | Capable of being done. Readable, comfortable, possible.
-ous/-ious | -ous | Having, full of, or characterized by. Famous, serious, courageous.
-al/-ial | -al | Relating to or of the nature of. Musical, natural, industrial.
-ic | -ic | Relating to or having the qualities of. Heroic, electric, historic.
-ive | -ive | Tending to or having the nature of. Active, creative, descriptive.
-ent/-ant | -ent | Forms adjectives meaning "doing" or "being" something, and agent nouns. Confident, dependent, resistant. Similar to -ive but from a different Latin stem class.
-ence/-ance | -ence | Forms abstract nouns from verbs or adjectives, referring to a state, quality, or action. Dependence, reference, interference. The noun counterpart to adjectives ending in -ent/-ant.
-ly | -ly | Forms adverbs from adjectives, meaning "in a [manner]." Quickly, happily, surprisingly. Also forms adjectives: friendly, lovely.
-ship | -ship | A state, condition, or relationship. Friendship, leadership, scholarship.
-hood | -hood | A state, condition, or group. Childhood, neighborhood, brotherhood.
-dom | -dom | A state, realm, or collective. Freedom, kingdom, wisdom.
-ward/-wards | -ward | In the direction of. Forward, backward, upward.
